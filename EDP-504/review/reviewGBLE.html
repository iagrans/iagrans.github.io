Game Based Learning Systems Quizlet

Game Based Learning systems are just one of the many technologies being created to enhance learning. Researchers continue to explore game design strategies to help develop the student learning and engagement to make educational video games successful. Click through these questions to review how cognitive science impacts the designs and developments of educational games.

How do games contribute to active learning?

View Answer

  • In video games players can manipulate, explore and interacti with objects and events that otherwise might be too expensive or dangerous for traditional classroom settings.

Question 3

Which of the following is not an example of scaffolding in Crystal Island?

  • the text message app that lets characters recommend resources for our player
  • the student first matching a picture of the island with a model, and then constructing a model theirselves
  • increasing motivation by allowing players to select their own avatar

View Answer

  • increasing motivation by allowing players to select their own avatar
  • Scaffolding provides supports for players by easing them into new learning concepts and then fading away.

Question 4

Which of the following can be learned through educational video games?

  • Topics in Math and Science that are well-defined
  • Social awareness for causes like green energy, animal cruelty, and peace in the Middle East
  • Simulation training for auto pilots, buisness, and other industry jobs

View Answer

  • All of the above are correct. Educational video games can be used to teach all kinds of things, however the more well-defined the problem space is, their is a higher tendancy to be successful.

Question 7 (bonus)

If a game presents an experienced user with basic hints and tips on how to play the game or solve a problem this can lead to Expertise Reversal Effect caused by...

View Answer

  • Redundancy of the material placing extraneous cognitive load on the player. This might help novices, but experts can be hurt by this.
