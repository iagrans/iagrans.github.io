Intelligent Tutoring Systems Quizlet

Intelligent tutoring systems are just one of the many technologies being created to enhance learning. Researchers continue to explore cognitive theories to help develop the student and learning models need to make intelligent tutors successful. Click through these questions to review how cognitive science impacts the designs and developments of Intelligent Tutoring Systems.

Question 3

Which of the following statements is/are correct?

  • Schema-like controlled processes are called nonrecurrent skills
  • Procedure-like automatic processes are called recurrent skills
  • Schema-like controlled processes are called recurrent skills and procedure-like automatic processes are called nonrecurrent skills

View Answer

  • Schema-like controlled processes are called nonrecurrent skills, because they are effortful and require focused attention.
  • Procedure-like automatic processes are called recurrent skills, as they require little or no conscious attention.

Question 7

What is an intelligent tutoring system?

View Answer

  • An intelligent tutoring system is a computer system that aims to provide immediate and customized instruction or feedback to learners, usually without intervention from a human teacher.
