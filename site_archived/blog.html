  • Day 1: Departure

    At 6:30 am I begin the more than 3,000 mile trip to Seattle, WA via DFW Airport. I enjoy all the perks of a middle sit on the first stretch there. Followed by remarkable luck of a premium window seat right behind first class.

    I landed around 2:00pm grabbed lunch and hopped a taxi to my apartment. Mr. Taxi driver and I enjoyed a nice round about way to my building where I found my room, a cute little setup and my food rations for the next 24 hours (granola bar, small bag of cookies, and pierre water). I spent the evening unpacking my stuff and searching Bing Maps for the closest signs of civilization (food, food store, stuff store). All of which are at least a 20 minute walk.

  • Day 2: The Rescue

    On my first full day where I had the chance to explore. I took it unpon myself to look unnecessarily kept. I spent 2.5 hours painting my toes, doing my hair, changin my outfit at least 3 times, and buffing my nails. After properly acessorizing, I stepped out into the bright outdoors, only to realize it was a mere 52 degrees out. It had happened that although the internet told me it would be 80 degrees that day, it was only 7:45 am and not yet warm. My East coast body clock the better of me.

    After waiting for the weather to warm up and my food rations now gone, I tried to make contact with the outside world. I called planet Katie, unfortunately her orbit was being delayed by the brunch belt. It would be 2.5 more hours until Katie would come to my rescue and shuttle me away to the land of survival supplies. After returning, we commenced in retail therapy for our generous hero.

    After a not so busy, busy day I returned home enjoyed the spoils of my food gathering. Mt roommate arrived at 9 an some change from Montreal CA, we said quick hellos and then I let her settle in and recover from jet lag.

  • Day 3: NEO

    For my first day work I was given the marching orders of show up by 8:15 for paperwork. Not having free transportation options other than my own two feet, I decided to walk the 35 minutes to work. I made it ~ half way there, when I met another new hire, who was trying to catch a shuttle the rest of the way. Our luck with the shuttle was not great, but a wonderful employee who saw the well dressed man I was with and my "winning" smile offered us a ride.

    After having alotted extra time for myself to get their via feet, I was now there ~30 minutes early. This would start what would become the waiting game of NEO. NEO wouldn't actually let our group in until 8:30, once we got through the first set of gates, they directed to several other well hidden gates and check points. I eventually received my new swipe badge and headed to the orientation breakfast.

    Afterwards we listened to the standard saftey, ethics, and welcome intro that one would usually here. In addition to a fun bit on corprate policy and legalese. Lunch came around and our mentors came to pick us up like orphans and pound puppies. Each of us interns eagerly awaiting to see who our new mentor would be. I had the pleasure of enjoying a lovely email chain between my mentor who will be M.I.A this summer and her manager who would be collecting me. Apparently, there was confusion on where I would be collected from.

    The rest of the day consisted of me setting up my station and going through all of the 50+ emails I've ignored by not logging into my corprate email before this day. Whoops. I also realized that I am not familiar with Lync or Outlook and should so some intro lessons.

    I did walk the whole 35 minutes home. It wasn't too bad.
